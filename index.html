Early Years

I lived in Tondo's vibrant yet challenging streets at age 3 or 4. Though my mother rarely shares stories about those times, fragments of memories remain. I also recall making childhood friends in the neighborhood, playing outside, and even remembers scavenged meals from the trash that might be unconventional, but it evoked a sense of community and resilience. My father would surprise me with small toys, which didn't last. I remember staying in an abandoned building briefly, a memory that still lingers. I'm unsure how long we lived there, as my mother keeps those details private. However, images of our small home are etched in my mind. These memories, though incomplete, shaped a part of who I am today.

Moving to GrandParents

Moving to Central Luzon, in the province of Tarlac was a bit stark, though my family rarely shares about it, some memories remain vivid. I also recall enrolling school in that place, struggling with the dialect i didnt even last a sem. I remember how my grandparents love me so much that even when we are about to leave because of family problem, my grandaparents cries and beg for me to stay. I remember exploring the province briefly, and the memory still lingers. As my family keeps details private, and i barely remember anything coz im still a child back then. I also remembers some of my friends and cousin coz we use to play bicycle back then. I would love to come back to that place soon.

Hobbies

Travelling

Traveling is my passion, and I'm always eager to explore new destinations. For now, I'm excited to discover the hidden gems in my own province, where breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and warm hospitality await. From scenic coastal roads to majestic mountains, I'm looking forward to immersing myself in the local way of life, trying new foods, and capturing unforgettable memories. Whether it's a spontaneous road trip or a carefully planned itinerary, I know that each journey will bring its own unique adventures and experiences that will leave me feeling inspired and refreshed.

Building Hobby

As I've grown older, my hobbies have taken an unexpected turn. Despite spending my career studying computers and related fields, I've discovered a new passion for building things. With no formal training as a carpenter, I've had to learn through trial and error. I'd search online tutorials, make mistakes, and redo things until I got it right. The process has been exhilarating, and the sense of accomplishment I feel when I complete a project is unmatched. I've become surprisingly self-sufficient, tackling projects like building a fishpond, outdoor kitchen, parking space, and even piggery cages. Without needing to hire a carpenter, I've taken on the challenge and learned as I go. It's been a rewarding journey, and I'm proud of what I've created with my own two hands.

Piggery Farming

Pig farming is more than just a hobby for me - it's a labor of love that brings both joy and challenge. Despite the many obstacles and discouragements that come with it, I find myself drawn to the simple yet rewarding process of raising these intelligent and social animals. For now, it's a passion project that allows me to connect with nature and learn new skills. But I envision a future where it becomes a sustainable source of income for me and my family. With every hurdle, I take a deep breath, close my eyes, and press on, fueled by the hope of partial success and the thrill of nurturing life. Even if it starts as just a hobby, I'm excited to see where this journey takes me.
